About Me

Hello! I am a passionate researcher focusing on the atmospheric composition of exoplanets and their potential for habitability. My research involves spectroscopic observations, numerical modeling, and exploring indicators of clouds and hazes in exoplanet atmospheres.

I am currently a Project Fellow at the National Institute of Science Education and Research (NISER), working on advanced atmospheric modeling techniques.

Projects

Here are some of my recent and ongoing projects:

  • Exoplanet Atmosphere Modeling: Developing state-of-the-art models to simulate atmospheric spectra and cloud formations.
  • Spectroscopic Observations: Analyzing data from space telescopes to identify molecular signatures.
  • Habitability Assessment: Investigating environmental conditions that support life.
